# WVG Brand Guidelines

## 1. Brand Essence

### Core Identity
- Scrappy, bold
- Technical
- Editorial
- Confident
- Venture-driven

### Visual Identity
- Dark-first design
- High-contrast typography
- Structured layouts
- Subtle gradients
- Urban imagery

### Design Goal
Create a venture-grade aesthetic that blends editorial design, technical precision, and modern venture identity.

---

## 2. Color System

### Base Colors

#### Black
- HEX: #000000
- Usage: Primary background

#### White
- HEX: #FFFFFF
- Usage: Primary text and borders

### Accent Colors

#### Green
- HEX: #12B378

#### Teal
- HEX: #11C1BC

#### Blue
- HEX: #004AAD

#### Purple
- HEX: #E385EC

### Color Usage Rules
- Black dominates the UI
- White used for text and structural lines
- Accent colors used sparingly
- Gradients combine green, teal, blue, purple
- Avoid colors outside the defined palette

### Gradient Style
- Shape: Circular swirl or wave
- Opacity: Low to medium
- Usage: Backgrounds, highlights, transitions

---

## 3. Typography System

### Primary Serif (Headlines)

#### Font
- Times New Roman Condensed or equivalent serif

#### Usage
- Hero headlines
- Key statements

#### Style Rules
- Large scale
- Tight tracking
- Editorial tone

---

### Sans-serif (Subheadings)

#### Font
- CY Grotesk or equivalent grotesk

#### Usage
- Section titles
- UI labels

#### Style Rules
- Bold
- Uppercase optional

---

### Monospace (Body and Metadata)

#### Font
- Roboto Mono or equivalent monospace

#### Usage
- Body text
- Captions
- Metadata
- UI microcopy

#### Style Rules
- Small size
- High readability

---

### Typography Hierarchy

#### H1
- Serif
- Very large

#### H2
- Sans-serif
- Bold

#### Body
- Monospace
- Small

#### Meta
- Monospace
- Smaller

---

### Grid

- Modular sections

### Section Patterns

#### Pattern A
- Text left
- Visual right

#### Pattern B
- Visual left
- Text right

#### Pattern C
- Full-width editorial panel

### Principles

## 8. Voice and Copy

### Tone
- Direct
- Editorial
- Technical

### Style
- Short sentences
- No marketing fluff

### Examples

#### Bad
“We empower founders with innovative solutions.”

#### Good
“A venture community built in Waterloo.”

---

## 9. Do and Do Not

### Do
- Use black as primary canvas
- Use serif headlines sparingly but boldly
- Use monospace for technical feel
- Maintain strong hierarchy

### Do Not
- Use playful fonts
- Use bright white backgrounds as default
- Add heavy shadows
- Overload with colors

---

## 10. Website Implementation Rules

### Hero Section

#### Required Elements
- Black background
- Large serif headline
- Monospace subtitle
- City image or gradient motif

---

### Sections
Alternate between:
- Text-heavy editorial panels
- Visual-heavy panels

---

### Spacing
- Large margins
- Clear hierarchy
- Breathing room

---

## 11. AI Design Instructions

### Rules for Cursor, GPT, v0, and Design Tools
- Default background: black
- Default text: white
- Serif headlines, monospace body text
- Thin white borders and grid lines
- Grayscale city imagery
- Subtle green, teal, blue, purple gradients
- Editorial layouts with asymmetry
- Avoid playful or colorful UI patterns
